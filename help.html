Quick Answers

Common questions answered instantly. Click to expand.

If you forgot your ID, you may still be able to vote using a provisional ballot. Check your state's rules or ask a poll worker. Some states allow signature verification or other forms of identification instead.

Yes, but check the registration deadline for your new address. You may need to update your registration before election day. Use your state's election website to verify your current registration status and update if needed.

All polling places must provide accessibility accommodations by law. These include wheelchair access, voting machines with audio assistance, curbside voting, and language support. Check the Logistics page to see what services are available at your station.

You can ask a poll worker for help reading the ballot or understanding issues. You can also bring notes or a printed copy of your ballot choices into the voting booth. Check the Candidates and Ballot pages to prepare before election day.
